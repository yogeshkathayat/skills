# LinkedIn Launch Timing + Engagement Runbook

Load at Steps 4 & 8. The golden hour + replying to every comment is where a LinkedIn launch is won.

## When to post

**Day: Tuesday–Thursday (Wednesday is strongest in fresh data).** **Time: test morning vs afternoon for
your audience** — the classic "8–11am B2B morning" default is now outdated. 2026 datasets shifted later:
Sprout recommends **Tue–Thu 11am–5pm**; Buffer's 4.8M-post study found **afternoon/evening (3–8pm)** often
out-engages mornings (top slots Wed 4pm, Fri 3pm/4pm). LinkedIn itself declines a single best time. **Pick
a weekday slot your audience is awake for, then win the first 60–90 minutes** — the exact minute matters
less than it used to (LinkedIn briefly resurfaced older relevant posts in mid-2025, then partly rolled it
back, so relevance softens recency *somewhat*, not fully).

## The golden hour

LinkedIn shows a new post to a small sample (~2–5% of network, est.) and **early engagement velocity in
roughly the first 60–90 minutes** decides whether it expands to 2nd/3rd-degree feeds; an under-performing
hour-one post rarely recovers. **Comments and dwell** are what advance it — dwell time is an **officially
confirmed** ranking signal (LinkedIn engineering). So be at your desk for the first 90 minutes.

## The top repeatable lever: reply to every comment

**Replying to comments lifts engagement ~30%** (~83% of accounts improved; ~72k-post Buffer study — the
strongest evidence here). Your reply is itself a comment (adds count + dwell), pulls the commenter back for
a second visit, and builds the back-and-forth thread the algorithm rewards. **Reply substantively, never
"Thanks!"**:

- *Follow-up question:* "Good point on [X] — how are you handling [edge case] on your side?"
- *Add a data point:* "Agreed. We saw [specific result] when we tried this — here's the breakdown."
- *Build on their experience:* "That matches what we hit with [scenario]. The surprise was [insight]. Same
  for you?"

Long, specific replies help (more dwell + word count); the constraint is relevance, not length.

## Team amplification — SANCTIONED on LinkedIn (vs banned pods)

Employee amplification is **LinkedIn's own first-party tool** and is categorically different from an
engagement pod:

- **Page admins can "Notify Employees" of a post once per day** (official; employees can opt out).
  Fire it within minutes of publishing the post that matters, inside the golden hour. Don't spray it on
  every post (people opt out).
- Ask your **5–15 genuinely relevant** colleagues to react **in their own words** — employees adding
  personal commentary vastly out-perform copy-paste reshares and avoid looking pod-like.

**The compliance line:** ask people to engage **only if the post is genuinely relevant to them**, ask for
**their own words / a real reaction** (never a scripted comment), and **never** promise reciprocal likes or
run a fixed reciprocal schedule. That's the line between **employee advocacy (fine)** and an **engagement
pod (a ToS violation LinkedIn detects via reciprocal-timing — ~90% reach drops)** — see
`antipatterns-compliance.md`.

**Compliant teammate ask (paste-ready):**
> Hi [Name] — I just posted on [specific topic] that's directly relevant to your work on [their area]. If
> it genuinely resonates, I'd love your take in the comments — a real reaction, a counterpoint, or an
> example from your experience is far more valuable than a quick like. No pressure if it's not your thing.
> Link: [URL]

## Reposting & follow-up (for time zones)

Repost hierarchy by reach: original post (~28 median likes) ≫ **repost-with-thoughts** (~8) ≫ bare repost
(~2). Default to a **repost-with-thoughts** (100–180 words of *new*, experience-based commentary + a
question — don't echo the original's first 30 words) on your best content **12–24h later** to catch other
time zones; a bare repost is a weak signal boost only.

## First-day runbook

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| **T−15 min** | Line up 5–15 genuinely-relevant colleagues; draft your own first comment (a question or extra resource). |
| **T+0 (publish)** | Post in a weekday slot your audience is online (default Tue/Wed/Thu; test AM vs PM). |
| **T+0–5 min** | Admin fires Page **"Notify Employees"** (1/day); DM the 5–15 colleagues the relevance-framed ask; post your **first comment with the link**. |
| **T+0–90 min (golden hour)** | Stay live. Reply substantively to **every** comment (the ~30% lever); engage every reactor. No "thanks". |
| **T+2–4h** | Keep replying to trickle-in comments. |
| **T+8–12h** | Optional bare repost for another time zone. |
| **T+12–24h** | **Repost-with-thoughts** (100–180 words new commentary) on top content. |
| **Day 2 (post-launch, Step 8)** | Sweep stragglers; reply by adding to their point; have the company page re-share; repurpose the best moments. |
