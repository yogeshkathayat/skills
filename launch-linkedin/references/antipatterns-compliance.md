# LinkedIn Launch Anti-Patterns & Compliance

Load at Steps 5 & 7. Like X (and unlike Product Hunt / Hacker News), **asking your network/team to engage
is allowed.** The constraints split into a **rule line** (LinkedIn policy — engagement bait and pods are
named violations), an **optimization line** (reach hurts), and what's **fine**.

## The one-liner

- **RULE LINE (LinkedIn policy — official):** **engagement bait** ("Comment YES if you agree", "Tag 3
  people", reaction-polls-for-reach, "double-tap if…") is classified as **spam**; **engagement pods** /
  any **pre-arranged reciprocal** like/comment/share swap is **prohibited**; automation tools/extensions
  for comments are detected and suppressed.
- **OPTIMIZATION LINE (legal but hurts reach — mostly third-party):** an external **link-preview card** in
  the body, 10+ hashtags / `#like #follow #comment`, **editing in the first ~60–90 min**, **2+ posts/day**
  (cannibalization), and an overtly **salesy** tone.
- **FINE (don't over-correct):** asking your **network/team** to **genuinely** read and react; the link in
  the **first comment**; 3–5 relevant hashtags; a **genuine question** as the CTA; posting consistently.

## Rule line (official policy — verbatim)

- **Engagement bait = spam.** LinkedIn's Spam help page lists *"Emoji / reaction polls that artificially
  boost engagement"*, *"posts that misrepresent the functionality of the LinkedIn platform … to
  artificially increase engagement"*, and *"widely circulated 'chain letter'-type content, requesting
  likes, reactions, and shares."* So "Comment YES", "Tag 3 people", "Like for part 2" are out. **Genuine
  open questions are NOT penalized** — the policy targets artificial metric inflation, not real conversation.
- **Engagement pods = prohibited.** Professional Community Policies: *"Don't do things to artificially
  increase engagement with your content. Respond authentically to others' content and **don't agree with
  others ahead of time to like or re-share each other's content.**"* The violation is the **pre-arranged
  reciprocity**, not that people you know engaged.
- **Enforcement is active and escalating:** Aug 2025 — LinkedIn may *"limit the visibility"* of automated /
  excessive comments; Feb 2026 (VP of Product, Gyanda Sachdeva) — automated comments are removed from "Most
  Relevant", restricted to the commenter's direct connections, and repeat offenders face **account
  restrictions**. Pod tooling (e.g. Lempod) was removed from the Chrome Web Store.

## Pods vs. team amplification (the line to teach)

- **Banned (pod):** a Slack/Telegram group that agrees to like+comment on each other's posts within 10
  minutes of publishing (pre-arranged, reciprocal, scheduled — detectable via timing/ordering/reciprocity).
- **Fine (advocacy):** Slacking your 8 teammates *"we launched, take a look if it's relevant to you,"* and
  they genuinely read and react **in their own words** — plus the official **"Notify Employees"** Page tool
  (once/day). The difference is the **pre-agreement to reciprocate**, not that people you know engaged. See
  `timing-engagement.md`.

## DO / DON'T (the launch checklist)

**✅ DO**
- Write a post that delivers the core insight **without requiring a click**.
- Put the link in the **first comment** (say "link in the comments"); or a plain-text URL near the end —
  **not** an attached preview card.
- End with a **genuine question** (real discussion prompt — fine; only fake-metric bait is penalized).
- Ask your **team/network to genuinely** read and react (their own words; the "Notify Employees" tool).
- Reply to **every** early comment yourself; use **3–5** relevant hashtags; keep **~80/20 value-to-promo**.
- **Proofread before publishing** so you don't need to edit in the momentum window.

**❌ DON'T**
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES", "Tag 3 people", reaction-polls-for-reach, "double-tap if…").
- Engagement pods or any pre-arranged like/comment/share swap; comment-automation tools/plugins.
- Attach a link-preview **card** in the body; 10+ hashtags or `#like #follow #comment`.
- **Edit** the post in the first ~60–90 min (especially adding a link/rewriting).
- Post **2+ times the same day** (cannibalizes reach — space ~24h).
- Make the whole post a sales pitch ("Buy now / Limited offer").

## The bait/pod scan (run over the post + any team ask)

Flag (case-insensitive) — any hit = rewrite: `comment YES`, `comment "`, `tag 3`, `tag a friend`, `like for
part`, `double-tap`, `drop a 🔥`, `#like`, `#follow`, `#commentbelow`, and any plan for **pre-arranged
reciprocal** engagement / a pod / a comment-automation tool. (A genuine open question and a real "take a
look if it's relevant" team ask are **not** violations.)
