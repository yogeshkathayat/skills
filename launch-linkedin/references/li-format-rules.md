# LinkedIn Launch Format Rules

Load at Step 3. The copy/skeleton lives in `post-copy.md`; this is the **format, limits, the hook, the
link nuance, hashtags, and format choice.**

## Limits & the hook

- **3,000 characters** per feed post (official — counts everything; identical mobile/desktop).
- Only the **first ~210 chars (desktop) / ~140 chars (mobile)** show before **"…see more"**. Most traffic
  is mobile, so **design the hook for the ~140-char / first-2-line budget** — that "see more" click is
  itself a positive engagement signal.
- **Optimal full length ~1,300–2,000 chars** (studies vary), formatted with **whitespace and one idea per
  line** for dwell. Short paragraphs and simple words read better. Lead with the hook; never open with
  "I'm excited to announce…".

## The link nuance (important — it's the CARD, not the URL)

The reach hit on LinkedIn comes from the **rich link-preview CARD**, not the link itself. In a 566k-post
study, an attached **preview card** scored the lowest median impressions of any format (~414), but a
**plain-text URL in the body** scored *higher* than a link-free post (~858 vs ~786). van der Blom's 2026
data puts a single in-body link at ~−18.8% median reach; others say more; LinkedIn's own Senior Director of
Product disputes any link penalty. **It's genuinely contested.**

**Safe default:** keep the body a native, value-first post and **put the launch link in the FIRST COMMENT**
("link in the comments"). If you'd rather keep it in the post, use a **plain-text URL near the end** (not an
attached preview card). **Don't over-engineer it** — format, hook, and dwell matter far more than where the
link sits. (The "LinkedIn detects bridge-to-comment behaviour" claim is unverified.)

## Format choice (drives dwell — pick one)

1. **Document / carousel PDF** — the **top-engagement format** (~6.6%; +~39% reach in a 3M-post study);
   8–12 slides telling a visual story (hook → problem → before/after → the 3 things it does → proof → CTA).
2. **A strong single image** — the best of the simple formats by median impressions (product shot /
   before-after / a result screenshot).
3. **Native video** — uploaded to LinkedIn (never a YouTube link); mixed reach on personal profiles unless
   3+ min, but strong for engagement; great from a company page.
4. **Text + one image** — fine if the text is substantive.

**Avoid** the attached **link-preview card** as the post's media.

## Hashtags

Use **~3 relevant, mostly niche** hashtags (one broad + two niche), placed at the end. More isn't better —
**3+ can underperform fewer**, and **10+ triggers spam suppression**. The thing that actually teaches the
algorithm your topic is **keyword-rich body copy**, not the hashtags.

## Where to post from

**Launch from the founder's PERSONAL profile, not the company page** — personal profiles get the lion's
share of feed allocation (~65% vs ~5% for company pages). Have the **company page re-share** afterward, not
the reverse. Tag **1–3 genuinely relevant people** + the company Page (irrelevant tagging hurts).

## Structure

**Problem-first, not product-first:** `HOOK (a felt problem / sharp number / personal-story opener — name
the product later)` → `STORY/PROBLEM` → `WHAT WE BUILT` → `PROOF (one specific result)` → `CTA (one soft
ask that drives a comment or a free action)`. Treat the launch as a **3–5 post campaign** across the week,
not one post. Full skeleton in `post-copy.md`.
